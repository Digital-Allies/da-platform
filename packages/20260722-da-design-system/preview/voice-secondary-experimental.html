Experimental Direction

Voice,
Reconsidered

A lighter reference for a voice that's still Digital Allies — just less rigid about it. This drops the split between two registers in favor of one warmer, more conversational voice, and swaps the invented department names for plain-language services.

What changed: The old system ran a stricter "primary" voice everywhere and saved this warmer register for marketing only. This version leads with the warmer register across the board, drops the Jargon Jar translation table, and drops the proprietary department names (The Departments, The Design Bureau, etc.) in favor of just calling things what they are — Services.

The Voice

Real talk about real problems, from someone who actually answers the phone. Warmer and more conversational than a typical services site, still direct, still honest — just not so buttoned-up about it.

In One Sentence

"Real talk about real problems from someone who actually answers the phone."

Still Avoid

"We facilitate seamless API-level ecosystem integration."
"We're passionate about nurturing your digital journey."
"Leverage scalable, user-centric digital solutions."
Anything that sends someone to a dictionary.

Loose Guidelines

Guardrails, not rules. Use judgment over checklists.

Keep

  • First person, direct address — "I" and "you"
  • Plain words over impressive ones
  • Owner: Anthony · Kingman, Arizona
  • Prices as "From $X"

Loosen

  • Casing and punctuation rules — sentence case is a default, not a law
  • Bracketed CTAs — use where they feel natural, skip where they don't
  • No more invented department names — see Services below
  • No more Jargon Jar translation table — just write plainly the first time
