Content Philosophy

The Format: Article + Social Pair
  • Each week anchors on one long-form article (600–900 words) published to your site or a platform like Medium/LinkedIn Articles.
  • Every article generates 3–4 social posts — teaser, excerpt, insight pull, and a CTA closer at the end of the week.
  • Posts always reference the article with a link — you're not just posting, you're building a destination.
  • Visual assets (cover photos, animated previews) are the scroll-stoppers that get people to click.
Platform Priority
  • LinkedIn — primary. Long-form posts do well here. Best for client acquisition.
  • Facebook — secondary. Kingman community content will land especially well here. Local groups are gold.
  • Instagram — visual-first. Cover photos and animated site previews go here. Less copy, more impact.
  • X/Twitter — optional. If you want to thread the AI content, this is where it lives.

Design System Reminders

Colors
Brand Palette
Cobalt Blue #2A4DF4 · Ink Black #0F1115 · Sand #E7E2D6 · Teal accent #7FDBCA (AI track) · Amber accent #F4A62A (Kingman track). Never use all five at once — pick two or three per piece.
Typography
Type Hierarchy
Headlines: DM Serif Display (or your Söhne Breit if available). Body / labels: DM Mono or Figtree. Never use more than two typefaces per asset. Let the serif do the heavy lifting on emotional impact.
Logo Usage
Wordmark Placement
Always bottom right or bottom left. Never centered. Small — 10–12% of asset width. White on dark, Ink Black on sand. Give it breathing room. This is a brand-builder month, so consistency matters more than size.
Automation Playbook
How to set this up once and let it run while you focus on other things.
Option A — Buffer (Recommended for simplicity)
  • Create a Buffer account (free tier covers 3 channels + 10 scheduled posts).
  • Connect LinkedIn, Facebook Page, and Instagram.
  • Load all 16 posts at once — one Sunday night session at the start of the month.
  • Set publish times: Mon 8am (article announce), Wed/Thu 11am (mid-week), Fri 4pm (closer).
  • Articles need to be live before Monday morning — write and publish Thursday/Friday prior.
  • Check in once a week for ~15 minutes to respond to comments and adjust if anything breaks.
Option B — Zapier (If you want deeper automation)
  • Trigger: New post published on your website/blog (RSS feed or CMS webhook).
  • Action: Auto-create a draft Buffer post with the article title + link.
  • You review and schedule — removes the copy-paste step entirely.
  • Add a second Zap: New Buffer post published → Log to a Google Sheet for tracking.
  • Optional: Zapier + Claude API to auto-generate social copy from article text.
  • This setup takes 2–3 hours to build but saves hours every month after that.

Article Writing Workflow

The 90-Minute Article Method
  • Use this framework as your brief — the outline is already written for each article.
  • Open Claude and paste the outline + hook. Ask for a first draft in your voice.
  • Read it aloud. Edit for anything that doesn't sound like you. This is the most important step.
  • Add one specific personal detail per article — a real moment, a real observation. That's what makes it yours.
  • Publish to your site. Copy the URL. That's your link for the week.
  • Total time target: 90 minutes per article including edits.
Monthly Reset Checklist
  • Week before launch: Write and schedule all 4 articles. Build cover photos in Canva.
  • Sunday before Month Start: Load all social posts into Buffer with times.
  • Every Monday: Confirm the week's article is live before 8am.
  • Every Friday: Spend 15 min reviewing engagement, note what landed.
  • End of Month: Pull Buffer analytics. Note top-performing post format. Use that as your guide for Month 2.
