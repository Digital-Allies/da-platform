# Digital Allies — Brand Voice Guide
**"Your Smartest Friend Who Happens to Know Technology"**

---

## 1. Voice Summary

Digital Allies sounds like a sharp, unhurried person who picks up the phone, tells you the truth, and doesn't make you feel dumb for asking. The wit is real, not performed. The warmth shows in what we do — and occasionally in what we say. We're direct, a little irreverent, and genuinely invested in the outcome.

Think: your smartest friend who happens to know technology, explaining things over coffee.

---

## 2. Voice Principles

**1. Say the smart thing simply.**
- ✅ DO: "Your apps talk to each other. You don't have to."
- ❌ DON'T: "We facilitate seamless API-level ecosystem integration."

**2. Let care show — don't perform it.**
- ✅ DO: "We answer the phone. In person. Every time."
- ❌ DON'T: "We're passionate about nurturing your digital journey." *(garden metaphor, discarded)*

**3. Be irreverent, not aloof.**
- ✅ DO: "Repetitive tasks are for machines. Go take a real lunch break."
- ❌ DON'T: "We leverage automation frameworks to optimize operational bandwidth." *(double violation)*

**4. Own the expertise without the posturing.**
- ✅ DO: "This is what we do all day. It won't take long."
- ❌ DON'T: "As a strategic technology partner, we align solutions to your roadmap."

**5. Match the moment.**
- ✅ DO: Keep a service email warm but efficient. Save the dry wit for website copy and social.
- ❌ DON'T: Deploy the Jargon Jar in a billing email. Read the room.

## 3. The Jargon Jar

*Corporate speak, translated.*

| They Say | We Say |
|---|---|
| Synergy | Working together (without the posturing) |
| Scalable | It won't break when more than three people show up |
| Deep Dive | Looking at it for more than five minutes |
| Bandwidth | Time. Just regular human time. |
| User-Centric | I put the buttons where your thumb can actually reach them |
| Leverage | To use. (Preferred by people who want to sound more expensive.) |
| Disruptive | Actually useful |
| Ecosystem | Your tools, working together |
| Pivot | Changed our minds. On purpose. |
| Onboarding | Getting started |

---

## 4. Tone Spectrum

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Website copy** | Confident, dry wit, a little editorial | "The Dept. of Cooperation: your apps, finally on speaking terms." |
| **Social posts** | Warmer, shorter, direct — occasionally a one-liner | "We watch the servers. You watch the sunset." |
| **Client emails** | Warm and efficient. Wit optional, competence required. | "Here's what I found, here's what I'm doing about it, here's when it'll be done." |
| **Error messages** | Honest and calm — never cute, never cold | "Something went sideways. We're on it — or email hello@digitalallies.com." |
| **Proposals** | Plainspoken authority. No fluff. | "Here's what you need, why it works, and what it costs." |

## 5. Sample Lines — Unified Voice

> *"Repetitive tasks are for machines. Go have a sandwich."*

**Unified voice:**
> "Repetitive tasks are for machines. You've got better things to do — and we'll make sure the machines know it."
