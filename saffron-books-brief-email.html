Overview

Melissa owns Saffron Books, a local bookstore on Route 66 in Kingman, AZ. She already runs Shopify for inventory, and it's hard-synced to her physical register. The goal is to build a public-facing website that connects to her existing Shopify setup — without disrupting what's already working or adding recurring software costs.

The preview site linked above shows the design direction and layout built for this proposal.

The Problem

Melissa's store has real character. A generic website won't reflect that — and rebuilding her inventory system from scratch would break the sync she already depends on at the register.

She needs a site that:

  • Pulls her live Shopify inventory without API middleware or subscription tools
  • Feels like a neighborhood bookstore, not a dropshipping template
  • Supports local search visibility and long-term customer retention
The Approach

No-API Shopify Bridge

We connect her Shopify inventory to the site using Shopify's free Buy Button sales channel — no third-party sync tools, no ongoing API costs.

Step 1 — Enable the Buy Button in Shopify

Inside Shopify Admin, we activate the free Buy Button sales channel. We generate a Collection Buy Button embed for an entire category (e.g., Staff Picks) rather than per-product codes. One embed covers the whole collection.

Step 2 — Style it to match the site

We configure the embed to use matching typography and colors. Checkout is set to open as a pop-up cart so customers never leave the page and land on a shopify.com subdomain.

Step 3 — Drop it into the site

We place the embed inside an HTML widget on the site. Container padding is set to 0px so Shopify's responsive grid aligns cleanly with the page layout. Paste, save, done.

Why this works: Her brick-and-mortar register stays the single source of truth for stock. No sync lag, no middleware, no added monthly cost.
What the Site Includes

Header — Clean navigation with an EN/ES language toggle and a live inventory status indicator.

Hero — Direct headline copy positioned around her Route 66 location and hand-picked inventory.

Live Inventory Section — Structured layout where the Shopify Buy Button embeds render, organized by collection. Updates automatically when Shopify updates.

Curator's Notebook — A minimal blog/review grid for short staff recommendations — two sentences per book, no filler.

Beyond the Website
Phase 1 — Local Search

We clean up her Google Business Profile so it accurately reflects her hours and location. We add a direct link pointing to her live inventory page rather than just the homepage. We also inject BookStore schema markup into the site header with geo-coordinates for Kingman — so voice assistants and AI search tools surface her physical location accurately.

Phase 2 — Email and In-Store Retention

We set up a single-field newsletter signup through MailerLite or Shopify Email. The format is a bi-weekly dispatch — three book picks, no sales pressure. We also design a simple printed bookmark with a QR code pointing to her Staff Picks page, bridging foot traffic into digital without asking anyone to follow anything.

Key Considerations

  • Checkout experience is Shopify-hosted; the cart pop-up keeps customers on-site but the final checkout page is Shopify's

  • Inventory sync is real-time through the embed — no scheduled jobs needed
