Context

About West of 3rd

West of 3rd is one of the most distinctive stops on Beale Street — a women-owned cooperative market at 224 E Beale St that brings together three in-house brands under one roof:

Southwest Trading Co. Wet Dirt. Rosebird Farms

Together they offer handcrafted jewelry, herbal body care, an apothecary, locally sourced produce, raw milk, pasture-raised meat, and a curated selection of artisan goods — most sourced directly from local makers, gardeners, and creators. It's a 4.9-star operation with a deeply loyal following.

You're currently on Square Online, which does the job. But with a storefront renewal underway, this is the right moment to make sure the digital presence matches what you're building in person.

The Timing

A Storefront Renewal Is the Right Moment

Storefront renovations change how people see a business — but that shift only works if the digital side keeps up. New signage, a refreshed layout, a better customer experience inside: all of that gets undermined when the website still looks like a starter Square template.

I'm not suggesting you abandon what's working. Square POS is solid and I'm not touching it. What I'm proposing is a proper public-facing site that matches the quality of what West of 3rd is actually becoming — one that shows up for tourists making decisions from the road, and for locals who find you through search before they walk through the door.

West of 3rd's cooperative model — three distinct brands sharing a single space — is actually a built-in content advantage. Most retailers have one story. You have three, and they all reinforce each other.
The Route 66 Reality

30 Seconds to Make the Decision

Tourists traveling Route 66 through Kingman are making micro-decisions constantly. The average window between "I wonder if there's anything worth stopping for" and "let's just keep driving" is about 30 seconds.

In that window, they're on their phone. They're checking Google, scanning photos, reading a single line of text. If West of 3rd doesn't show up — or shows up with a bare listing and no photos — that car keeps driving.

A real website, properly indexed, with rich local schema and visual inventory, gives West of 3rd a presence in that 30-second window. The Square site doesn't.

What shows up right now for "artisan market Kingman AZ"?

Mostly Yelp listings, partial Google entries, and sites that scraped your address. A proper site with schema markup and a Google Business update puts West of 3rd at the top — your words, your photos, your story.

Deliverables

What the Site Includes

Homepage A visual-first layout that leads with what makes West of 3rd different — the cooperative model, the local sourcing, and the artisan aesthetic. Designed to convert the 30-second scroll into a physical visit.
Brand Sections for Each Imprint Short dedicated sections for Southwest Trading Co., Wet Dirt., and Rosebird Farms — each with its own voice and visual identity, unified under the West of 3rd header.
