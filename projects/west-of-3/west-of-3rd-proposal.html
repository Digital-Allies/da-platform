Context

About West of 3rd

West of 3rd is one of the most distinctive stops on Beale Street — a women-owned cooperative market at 224 E Beale St that brings together three in-house brands under one roof:

Southwest Trading Co. Wet Dirt. Rosebird Farms

Together they offer handcrafted jewelry, herbal body care, an apothecary, locally sourced produce, raw milk, pasture-raised meat, and a curated selection of artisan goods — most sourced directly from local makers, gardeners, and creators. It's a 4.9-star operation with a deeply loyal following.

What I Can Add

A Site That Tells the Full Story

West of 3rd's cooperative model — three distinct brands sharing a single space — gives you something worth showing online. I can build a site that brings Southwest Trading Co., Wet Dirt., and Rosebird Farms together under one roof digitally the same way you've done it in person.

Square POS stays exactly as-is. The site I build sits alongside it — a public-facing presence for discovery, browsing, and connection, while your existing tools keep running the store.

Three complementary brands under one address is a story worth telling. I build each one its own space on the site while keeping the West of 3rd identity front and center.
Route 66 Visibility

Reaching Visitors Before They Arrive

Kingman sees a steady stream of Route 66 travelers making quick decisions about where to stop. I can make sure West of 3rd shows up for those searches — with your photos, your words, and your three-brand story — so visitors already know what to expect before they walk through the door.

I handle the local search setup: Google Business Profile, schema markup with your address and geo-coordinates, and category tagging for artisan market, apothecary, and local foods. The goal is for West of 3rd to appear accurately and completely wherever people are searching.
