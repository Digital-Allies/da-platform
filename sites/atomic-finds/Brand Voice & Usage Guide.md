# Atomic Fines — Brand Voice & Usage Guide

## The Brand in One Sentence

> *"Vintage rattan. Modern sensibility. Curated with love by Fran & Mabel."*

---

## Voice Characteristics

| Dimension | Position |
|-----------|----------|
| Formal ←→ **Casual** | 70% casual |
| Dry ←→ **Warm** | 80% warm |
| Technical ←→ **Plain** | 90% plain language |
| **Authentic** ←→ Performing | 100% authentic |
| **Expert** ←→ Eager | 85% confident, quietly expert |

The voice is **handmade, approachable, and unapologetically human**. It sounds like two friends who know their stuff and genuinely love what they do.

---

## Core Values in Copy

### 1. **Handcrafted Heritage**
Celebrate the story behind each piece. Every item has history, character, and earned patina.

**DO write:**
- "1970s peacock chair, lovingly restored to highlight its iconic lines."
- "Each piece tells a story of mid-century craftsmanship."
- "Handpicked from estate sales and vintage dealers across the country."

**DON'T write:**
- "Pre-owned furniture items"
- "Authentic vintage authenticity"
- "Gently used condition rating"

---

### 2. **Sustainable by Nature**
Emphasize that buying vintage is the ultimate sustainable choice—no waste, no guilt, just timeless design.

**DO write:**
- "Giving vintage rattan a second life."
- "Beautiful pieces built to last generations."
- "No new production. No waste. Just rattan that never goes out of style."

**DON'T write:**
- "Eco-friendly solutions"
- "Sustainable consumption metrics"
- "Carbon-neutral product"

---

### 3. **Fran & Mabel's Personality**
The business is built on the real people behind it. Honor their taste, expertise, and warmth.

**DO write:**
- "Fran & Mabel's Rattan Revival Retreat"
- "Curated by Fran & Mabel"
- "Found, restored, and loved by us."

**DON'T write:**
- "The Atomic Fines Team"
- "Our collection experts"
- "Professional vintage sourcing specialists"

---

## Tone by Context

### Product Descriptions
**Warm, detailed, conversational.** Show the reader why *they* need this piece.

> "This 1970s peacock chair is the statement piece every room deserves. The iconic fan-back design is instantly recognizable, and the natural rattan has that perfect patina that says 'I've been loved.' Ready to add authentic mid-century energy to your space—or make someone's year as a gift."

### Hero/Marketing Copy
**Bold, confident, inviting.** Lead with emotion and outcome.

> "Handpicked vintage rattan, restored with love. Each piece tells a story of mid-century craftsmanship and timeless design."

### About Page
**Personal, genuine, grounded.** Let Fran & Mabel's voice shine.

> "We started Atomic Fines because we couldn't stop collecting rattan. What began as a passion became a mission: to save the most beautiful pieces from being discarded, restore them with respect, and pass them on to people who'll cherish them as much as we do. No overnight success story. No venture capital. Just two people, a growing warehouse, and an unwavering belief that vintage furniture deserves a second life."

### FAQs
**Helpful, clear, occasionally witty.** Answer real questions like a friend would.

> **Q: How do you source your pieces?**  
> A: Estate sales, auctions, antique dealers, and occasionally someone's grandmother's attic. We've developed an eye for pieces with good bones and incredible bones. All of it gets cleaned, inspected, and restored by folks who actually understand vintage construction.

### Testimonials
**Authentic voice, no fluff.** Let real customers speak.

> "Found this chair at Atomic Fines and it completely changed my living room. The quality is incredible, and you can tell it's been cared for. 10/10 would recommend to anyone who loves vintage design." — Sarah, Portland

## Vocabulary & Terminology

Use **plain language first**. Reserve these brand terms:

| Preferred | Avoid |
|-----------|-------|
| Handpicked | Curated (overused) |
| Restored | Refurbished, reconditioned |
| Mid-century / 1970s / Era-specific | "Authentic vintage," "retro period" |
| Rattan weave, peacock chair, sunburst | Generic "woven," "statement seating" |
| Condition: Excellent / Very Good / Good / Fair | Pristine, like-new, lightly used |
| In Stock / Low Stock / Out of Stock | Available, limited availability |
| Featured Collection | Best sellers, top rated |

## The Atomic Aliens

The lime-green alien characters are **visual mascots of curiosity and whimsy**. They represent:
- **Playful discovery** — finding treasure in unexpected places
- **Wonder** — treating vintage design with awe and respect
- **Character** — each piece (and each alien) has personality

**Use them to:**
- Add visual lightness to dense content
- Highlight key features or callouts
- Signal the "fun" side of taking vintage seriously
- Make the brand feel approachable and young at heart

**DON'T use them to:**
- Diminish the craftsmanship or quality of pieces
- Cartoon-ify serious restoration work
- Make Fran & Mabel seem less expert

## CTA & Button Copy

**Use square brackets** and action verbs. Short, punchy, inviting.

| Button | Context |
|--------|---------|
| [ Explore Collection ] | Hero, general browsing |
| [ View Full Catalog ] | Category pages, end of article |
| [ Schedule Viewing ] | High-value pieces, premium items |
| [ Add to Cart ] | Product detail page |
| [ Learn Our Story ] | About section, first-time visitor |

## Email & Newsletter Tone

**Personal and timely.** Lead with the piece, not the sale.

> **Subject:** New Arrival: A Stunning 1950s Floor Lamp  
> 
> Hi Sarah,
> 
> We just restored a floor lamp from the 1950s that stopped us in our tracks. Brass frame, linen shade, and it actually works—which is rarer than you'd think.
> 
> If you're looking for that one perfect lighting piece, this might be it.
> 
> [ View the Lamp ]
> 
> —Fran & Mabel

## What NOT to Do

❌ **Corporate speak:** "Leveraging authentic vintage solutions for your lifestyle ecosystem"  
✅ **Instead:** "Real rattan. Real history. Real craftsmanship."

❌ **Overuse superlatives:** "The MOST AMAZING piece EVER"  
✅ **Instead:** "A stunning 1970s peacock chair with authentic character."

❌ **Fake urgency:** "Only 2 left!!!" / "Limited time offer!"  
✅ **Instead:** "In stock now. Once it's gone, it's gone." (Simple truth.)

❌ **Jargon:** "Artisanal restoration methodologies," "curated marketplace experience"  
✅ **Instead:** "Carefully restored by hand," "Browse our collection"

❌ **Mixing brands:** Atomic Fines voice + Digital Allies voice  
✅ **Instead:** Keep Atomic Fines warm and artisan; save the technical precision for back-end/admin.

---

## Punctuation & Style

- **Em-dashes (—)** for flow and emphasis: "Vintage rattan—the kind that lasts—meets modern sensibility."
- **Square brackets [ ]** for CTAs: "[ Explore the Collection ]"
- **Ellipses (…)** sparingly, for contemplation: "Handpicked. Restored. Ready to find its forever home…"
- **No emoji** in marketing copy. Let the aliens do the visual work.
- **Sentence case** for headers: "Handpicked vintage rattan, restored with love."
- **All caps for labels/eyebrows:** "FEATURED COLLECTION · LIMITED AVAILABILITY"

## Brand Partnerships & Collaborations

If featuring third-party products or collaborators, maintain voice consistency:

**DO:** "We partnered with [Partner] because their [values/quality] align with ours."  
**DON'T:** "Proud to announce a strategic alliance with [Partner]"

---

## Final Checklist

Before publishing any Atomic Fines copy, ask:

- ☑️ Does it sound like Fran & Mabel would say this?
- ☑️ Is it in plain language? (Would my grandmother understand it?)
- ☑️ Does it celebrate the *story* behind the piece, not just the transaction?
- ☑️ Am I being authentic or performing?
- ☑️ Would I be proud to see this in print?
